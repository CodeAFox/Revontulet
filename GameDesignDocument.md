# Revontulet 
### A puzzle game about how the Northern Lights came to be

#### Game concept
The game will be based on the Finnish mithology of how and why Northern Lights appear on the night sky. A rough summary is as follows;
A fox, whose fur is black as the night during the day, runs through the snowy fields at night. As they speed up, their fur catches on fire, becoming what we see as the Northern Lights.
Revontulet translates to "fox flames".

I want the game to be a cozy, 2d, pixel-art kind of puzzle game. Going by the MDA, the core aspects I want to appeal to are Challenge, Fantasy, and Narrative. If time permits, and I do get to create my own assets (I genuinely hope so) I would like to potentially include Sensation too as a sub-aspect to the game. The aesthetics I would like to go for are more like Stardew Valley's art style mixed with a winter-wonderland kind of setting. Animation-wise, I do not believe there will be much procedural animation and it will most likely be keyframe for the most part, however that shall remain to be seen.
The target audience, or who I'd believe would love to play this, would be people who genuinely enjoy folklore and being told said folklore through puzzles. I'd imagine maybe people who enjoy Stardew Valley might could enjoy this too.

#### Gameplay
The game would be from the perspective of the fox; walking around and interacting with the environment in different ways. Currently I have a few ideas for what the puzzles could look like, but I'm not yet sure if I am trying to aim too high or not;
1. First idea is to collect stars and order them into constellations. I like this idea as it introduces multiple options for the puzzle. It could be that the puzzle itself is figuring out the right constellation, but it is also a possibility that the collection of each star would be the challenge, which could also introduce the second idea into the mix;
2. As I mentioned I want this to be a game based on narrative. I do wish to tell a story, even if it's not much. It would be a fun puzzle if each star one has to collect would come with its own investigative work where the player would have to interact with the story and the mythology behind the game to figure out how to get each star. I was thinking of introducing side characters too for this potentially, however that would definitely mean more assets with better animation than just a paper laying on the floor. Technically it is not a problem, but it will definitely be harder to create my own assets for that if I do decide I'm insane enough to try hand drawing and animating everything.

#### Game elements
For now, as I am unsure how hard making this will be, I would like to aim for at least two constellations with different challenges that the player would have to figure out. 
A level would be in a closed off, area where the player cannot move forward until they complete the constellation. Upon completion, the path to the next area would open and they can walk trough. They will be able to go back if they wish, and potentially later on it might be nice to add some connectivity between some levels, where you can get clues from a previous level too.

#### Assets
Models, for now, will be placeholders, meaning I'll have to find some at least somewhat fitting pieces. As for sound design, I would like to include some softer background music as that would definitely add to the cozyness of the game. The sound of crackling snow and such will also need to be added as sound effects, and perhaps some fox laughs too.
